First: What AI Mental Health Tools Are NOT

Primary Intent Summary

AI mental health tools are supportive aids, not therapists. They help with mild-to-moderate symptoms but cannot replace clinical human relationships or handle acute psychological crises.

✦ ✦ ✦

The 5 Red Flags: AI Tools to Avoid

Avoid any AI tool that displays the following warning signs:

🚩

Deceptive Language

App labels itself a "therapist."

🚩

No Crisis Path

Missing links or redirects to emergency helplines.

🚩

Zero Trials

Lacks peer-reviewed evidence proving safety.

🚩

Predatory Privacy

Policies that allow sharing or selling of user data.

🚩

Human Discouragement

App actively advises against seeing a real therapist.

✦ ✦ ✦

How to Use AI Tools Responsibly

Use AI tools as supports, not replacements. Keep your clinical team involved.

1

Daily Mood Tracking

Log daily trends to share with your therapist, similar to a physical health wearable.

2

Between-Session Support

Use CBT chatbots to reinforce coping techniques and practice grounding exercises late at night.

3

Consult Your Doctor

Work with your therapist to integrate the app safely into your care routine.

4

Have a Crisis Plan

Establish emergency numbers beforehand; AI is not equipped to handle acute crises.
